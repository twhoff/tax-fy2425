# Tizzi Business Schedule Review

Related issue: `TaxFY2425-c6j.5`
Status: ready for H&R Block questions

## Bottom Line

H&R Block's Tizzi P8 schedule ties arithmetically: depreciation `$3,854` + Ring `$78` + subscriptions `$93` = business loss `$4,025`, and the same `$4,025` is deferred as a non-commercial business loss.

The main review points are timing, private-use apportionment, and double counting. The iPhone/Pencil invoice is dated 30 Jun 2025 but dispatched 1 Jul 2025, while ATO instant asset write-off guidance depends on the asset being first used or installed ready for use by 30 Jun 2025 for the 2024-25 claim. Replit also appears risky because P8 `SUB $93` looks like 50% of Replit AUD charges, while H&R Block D5 employee subscriptions also list Replit.

## Items Checked

- Business income: H&R Block shows `$0`, consistent with available household facts and no revenue evidence found in this slice.
- Business commencement: household instructions say Tizzi started 28 Jun 2024, while the H&R draft says commenced during the year. H&R Block should confirm the correct business start details.
- Depreciation: iPad `$2,148.99` plus iPhone/Pencil `$1,705.00` equals `$3,853.99`, rounded to H&R's `$3,854`.
- Ring: H&R's `$78` ties to the Ring doorbell only. The `$98` Ring camera and Ring subscription bank charges appear excluded.
- Subscriptions: H&R's `$93` appears to be 50% of Replit AUD bank charges `$185.35` rounded from `$92.68`.
- Deferred loss: With `$0` income and no evidence of passing the non-commercial loss tests, deferring the `$4,025` loss appears consistent, subject to any expense corrections.
